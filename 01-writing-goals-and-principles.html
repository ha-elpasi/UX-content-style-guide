Writing Goals and Principles

Good content helps users complete tasks quickly and confidently. With every piece of content we write, we aim to:

  • Be clear. Say exactly what you mean. If a user has to re-read a sentence, it needs rewriting.
  • Be concise. Cut every word that doesn’t earn its place. Short sentences are easier to scan, easier to translate, and harder to misread.
  • Be simple. Use plain language. If a simpler word exists, use it. Avoid jargon, technical terms, and abstractions unless they’re unavoidable.
  • Be useful. Before you write, ask: what does the user need to know right now? Give them that, and nothing more.
  • Be honest. Don’t overpromise or soften bad news with vague language. Users trust content that tells them the truth.

Every piece of content we publish should be:

  • Accurate. Only say things you can verify. Don’t guess.
  • Consistent. Use the same words for the same things across the product.
  • Accessible. Write for all users, regardless of their technical knowledge or reading level.
