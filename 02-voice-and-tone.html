Voice and Tone

Voice

Our voice is direct, clear, and human. We respect the user’s time and intelligence. We don’t pad, we don’t over-explain, and we don’t use three words where one will do.

When we write:

  • We are direct. We get to the point. Users are trying to complete a task, not read an essay. Lead with what matters.
  • We are plain. We use everyday language. No buzzwords, no jargon, no abstract nouns where a simple verb will do. For example, write “use” not “utilize,” and “help” not “facilitate.”
  • We are consistent. We use the same term for the same thing every time. Switching between “sign in” and “log in” in the same flow confuses users.

Tone

Our tone adapts to context, but clarity always comes first. A confirmation message can be warm. An error message should be calm and helpful. A security alert should be direct and factual.

When writing, consider what the user is trying to do and how they might be feeling. Match your tone to the moment.

Style tips

  • Active voice: Use active voice. Avoid passive voice.
  • Avoid slang and jargon: Write in plain English.
  • Write positively: Use positive language rather than negative language.
